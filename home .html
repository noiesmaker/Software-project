About Thrissur

Kerala, is a vibrant city located in the central part of the state. It is renowned for its deep-rooted traditions, historical significance, and grand festivals. The city is best known for the Thrissur Pooram, a spectacular temple festival held annually at the Vadakkunnathan Temple, featuring caparisoned elephants, traditional percussion music, and an awe-inspiring fireworks display. The Vadakkunnathan Temple itself is a prominent landmark and a UNESCO-recognized heritage site, showcasing classic Kerala-style architecture and ancient murals. Thrissur is also a hub of classical arts, being home to institutions like Kerala Kalamandalam, which nurtures performing arts such as Kathakali and Mohiniyattam. Economically, the city thrives as a major center for the gold jewelry trade and houses the headquarters of prominent banks like South Indian Bank. Tourist attractions like Athirappilly Waterfalls, Peechi Dam, and the nearby Guruvayur Temple make it a popular destination. Its educational institutions and healthcare centers further enhance its importance as a cultural and commercial center in Kerala.

Main Attractions

  • Thrissur Pooram: An iconic temple festival celebrated with majestic elephants, traditional percussion music, and fireworks.


  • Vadakkunnathan Temple: A sacred and ancient Shiva temple in the heart of the city.



  • Athirappilly Waterfalls: A breathtaking waterfall often referred to as the Niagara of South India.



  • Kerala Kalamandalam: A world-renowned institution for classical dance and music.

Culture and Heritage

Thrissur is home to prestigious cultural academies and a lively artistic scene. It is also known for its gold jewelry industry and is a hub for traditional Kerala art forms such as Kathakali and Mohiniyattam.

Religious Harmony

The city hosts sacred sites for all major religions, including the Guruvayur Temple, the Our Lady of Lourdes Cathedral, and various mosques and churches.

Travel Info

  • Nearest Airport: Cochin International Airport (about 50 km away)
  • Main Railway Station: Thrissur Railway Station
  • Languages: Malayalam, English
